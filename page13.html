Constraints, Risks, and Assumptions

Presenter: Joshua Noel D. Lo

Constraints

Budget Limitations

Limited financial resources may restrict scope of features

Time Constraints

Three-month timeline requires efficient execution

Resource Availability

Limited availability of specialized team members

Technology Dependencies

Reliance on external cloud services and QR technology

Data Privacy Requirements

Must comply with strict healthcare regulations

Key Risks

Data Security Breach

High

Unauthorized access to sensitive medical data

User Privacy Concerns

High

Public apprehension about sharing medical data

Website Downtime

High

System unavailability during critical emergencies

Low User Adoption

Medium

Lack of user acceptance or registration

Inaccurate Medical Info

Medium

Users provide incomplete or outdated information

Assumptions

QR Code Access

First responders and medical professionals will have access to devices capable of scanning QR codes

Validation: Verify with local emergency services if compatible devices are available

User Information Accuracy

Users will accurately input and maintain up-to-date medical information in their profiles

Validation: Clear reminders, warnings, and periodic update prompts

Medical Professional Adoption

Medical professionals will recognize value and integrate system into their protocols

Validation: Survey relevant medical groups to gauge interest

Proactively identifying constraints, risks, and assumptions allows us to develop mitigation strategies, validate critical assumptions, and establish realistic expectations for project success.

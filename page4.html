Introduction & Project Approach

Presenter: Jessie Noel Lapure

Introduction

MediAlert addresses the critical gap in emergency medical response by providing immediate access to vital medical information when seconds count.

By leveraging QR code technology on medical bracelets, we enable first responders to access patient histories instantly, improving treatment outcomes and potentially saving lives.

Need for the Project

Delayed Treatment: First responders lack critical information for immediate care decisions

Inappropriate Treatment: Unknown allergies or medications can lead to harmful interventions

Inefficient Response: Time wasted gathering information detracts from critical intervention

Existing Solutions: Current options like physical medical IDs are often lost, damaged, or inaccessible

Project Management Approach

A modified Waterfall methodology with iterative development elements for flexibility and continuous improvement.

Collaboration & Communication

Weekly status meetings, daily stand-ups, and collaborative project management tools

Quality Focus

Adherence to industry best practices, rigorous testing, and robust security measures

Risk Management

Proactive identification and mitigation of potential risks through comprehensive registers

Adaptive Planning

Flexibility to adapt to changing requirements and unforeseen challenges

Stakeholder Engagement

Active stakeholder involvement throughout the project lifecycle

MediAlert revolutionizes emergency care with a simple, affordable solution that makes critical medical information instantly accessible.
